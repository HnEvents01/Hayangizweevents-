SPRINTER

Opening Act | Woman in Poetry Showcase
▶ Watch SPRINTER's Performance

SPRINTER had the honor of opening the show for the headliners, setting the tone with her commanding voice and electrifying presence. Known for her rapid-fire delivery and lightning-quick wit, SPRINTER lived up to her name with a performance that left audiences breathless. Her opening set at the Woman in Poetry showcase was a whirlwind of words, emotions, and energy that showcased why she's one of the most exciting voices in contemporary poetry.

Most Remarkable Performance

SPRINTER's most powerful moment came with her poignant recitation about Gender-Based Violence (GBV). This emotionally charged poem addressed the harsh realities faced by women, delivered with such raw intensity that it moved many in the audience to tears. Her ability to tackle such difficult subject matter with both sensitivity and strength demonstrated her maturity as an artist.

SPRINTER opened with her signature piece, "Fast Lane," a poem about navigating the pressures of modern life at breakneck speed. True to her name, her delivery was fast yet crystal clear—every word perfectly articulated despite the rapid pace. This technical mastery demonstrated years of practice and refinement.

What makes SPRINTER's work particularly compelling is her ability to balance speed with substance. Her poem "Pause Button" was a beautiful contrast to her usual style—a slower, more reflective piece about the importance of finding moments of stillness in our chaotic lives. This versatility showed an artist in complete control of her craft.

The highlight of her set was undoubtedly "Word Sprint," an improvised piece where she took random words from the audience and wove them into a coherent, powerful poem on the spot. This display of mental agility and creative spontaneity brought the audience to their feet in thunderous applause.

SPRINTER's performance was a masterclass in pacing and energy management. She took the audience on an emotional journey that ranged from frenetic excitement to profound contemplation, proving that speed and depth are not mutually exclusive in poetic expression.
