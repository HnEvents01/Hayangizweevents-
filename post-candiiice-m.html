CANDIIICE.M: Unapologetically Bold and Brilliant

About the Artist

Tshegofatso Mahlare is a poet, presenter and theatre technician who was born on the 27nt of January 2003. Known as Candiiice.m, she is a first daughter to her mother following her big brother. She started off as a cultural dancer with the group "Bakgatlha Ba Mosetlha" at moretele, the North West province. They would perform at wedding events and some events hosted by the Bakgatlha King, Kgosi Nchaupe. There would be times where she would not only perform, but teach the dances at the orphanage, Kids Haven. She would volunteer at the orphanage whenever they had events or were going to trips. On the year 2024, she was a presenter for 4everstreamtv at the Thembisa 2nd Annual Awards ceremony which brought her closer to her presenting dreams. Other than being a poet,cultural dancer and presenter, Tshegofatso ventured into the world of theatre design and management which she is currently studying for at the Tshwane University Of Technology. She has stage managed "Echoes Of Ubuntu" alongside Sandra Rathogwa and "Emashidini Ka Nonjabulo". Tshegofatso Mahlare is considered an African and spirituality influenced artist as she has always been involved in plays that are rooted in those elements. She uses anything she can see as useful to portray a certain character or environment. Currently, she is working as a set designer and stage manager for one of the shows at the Tshwane University Of Technology, like she always says, "portraying a character is bringing it to life, we are the vessels of those that came before us".

Performance at The Big 5 Poetry Experience

Bringing an infectious energy to the stage, CANDIIICE.M delivered a performance that was equal parts hilarious, heartwarming, and hard-hitting. She had the audience roaring with laughter one moment and moved to contemplative silence the next. Her charisma and masterful command of the stage were undeniable, making her set a standout favorite. She left no doubt that she is a powerhouse poet with a message that demands to be heard.
